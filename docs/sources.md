# Academic Sources and Bibliography

## Primary Historical Sources (原始史料)

### Chinese Historical Texts

#### Han Dynasty (汉朝)
- **Hanshu 汉书 (History of the Former Han Dynasty)**
  - Author: Ban Gu 班固 (32-92 CE)
  - Sections: Treatise on Food and Money (食货志)
  - Digital Access: [Chinese Text Project](https://ctext.org/han-shu)
  - Key Data: Land tax rates, poll tax amounts, state monopolies

- **Hou Hanshu 后汉书 (History of the Later Han Dynasty)**
  - Author: Fan Ye 范晔 (398-445 CE)
  - Relevant Chapters: Economic Treatises
  - Key Data: Tax reforms, corvée labor requirements

#### Tang Dynasty (唐朝)
- **Tang Huiyao 唐会要 (Institutional History of Tang)**
  - Compiler: Wang Pu 王溥 (922-982)
  - Volumes: 42-48 (Taxation sections)
  - Key Data: Two-tax system (两税法), salt monopoly revenues

- **Jiu Tang Shu 旧唐书 (Old Book of Tang)**
  - Chapter 48: Treatise on Economics
  - Digital Access: Available through Academia Sinica databases

#### Song Dynasty (宋朝)
- **Song Huiyao Jigao 宋会要辑稿 (Collected Essentials of Song)**
  - Section: Fiscal Administration (食货)
  - Key Data: Commercial tax rates, government monopolies

- **Wenxian Tongkao 文献通考**
  - Author: Ma Duanlin 马端临 (1254-1323)
  - Comprehensive encyclopedia of Chinese institutions
  - Taxation sections: Juan 1-20

### Roman and Byzantine Sources
- **Res Gestae Divi Augusti**
  - Author: Emperor Augustus
  - Digital: [Perseus Digital Library](http://www.perseus.tufts.edu)

- **Codex Theodosianus**
  - Imperial tax legislation 438 CE
  - English Translation: Pharr, C. (1952)

## Archaeological Evidence

### Excavated Documents
1. **Juyan Han Slips (居延汉简)**
   - Location: Gansu Province
   - Period: Han Dynasty
   - Content: Tax records, administrative documents
   - Publication: Loewe, M. (1967) "Records of Han Administration"

2. **Dunhuang Manuscripts (敦煌文献)**
   - Period: Tang-Song
   - Tax-related documents: S.1156, P.2040, P.3018
   - Digital Archive: [International Dunhuang Project](http://idp.bl.uk/)

3. **Turfan Documents**
   - Period: Tang Dynasty
   - Tax receipts and household registers
   - Reference: Hansen, V. (1995) "Negotiating Daily Life in Traditional China"

## Data Verification Standards

### Reliability Ratings
- **High**: Multiple corroborating sources, archaeological evidence
- **Medium**: Single authoritative source, widely cited
- **Low**: Limited sources, contested interpretations

### Cross-Verification Requirements
1. Primary source citation required
2. Secondary academic analysis
3. Quantitative data validation
4. Temporal and regional context

## Citation Format

### Standard Format
```
Author Last, First (Year). "Article Title." Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pp. XX-XX.
DOI: 10.xxxx/xxxxx
[Database/Archive] URL if available
Data Points: [Specific data extracted]
Reliability: [High/Medium/Low]
```

### Example Citation
```
Ma, Debin (2014). "State capacity and great divergence." 
Economic History Review, 67(1), pp. 1-35.
DOI: 10.1111/1468-0289.12016
Data Points: Qing tax revenue 3-4% of GDP (p. 18)
Reliability: High (multiple sources, peer-reviewed)
```

## Contributing New Sources

To add new sources to this database:
1. Verify academic credentials (peer review, university affiliation)
2. Extract specific data points with page references
3. Assess reliability using our criteria
4. Submit via GitHub pull request with full citation
